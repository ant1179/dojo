# Product Vision

> This document defines the long-term vision of the product.
>
> It explains **why the product exists**, **who it serves**, and **what success looks like**.
>
> Every product decision should align with this vision.

---

# Product Name

> Working name of the product.

---

# Vision Statement

Describe the long-term ambition of the product.

A good vision should inspire decisions for several years.

Example:

> Build the simplest platform for managing personal finances without compromising user privacy.

---

# Mission

Describe what the product does every day.

The mission is concrete.

The vision is aspirational.

---

# Problem Statement

What problem are we solving?

Describe:

- current situation
- pain points
- existing alternatives
- why existing solutions are insufficient

---

# Target Users

Describe the primary users.

For each persona specify:

- Who they are
- Their goals
- Their frustrations
- Their technical level

---

# Value Proposition

Why would someone choose this product?

Examples:

- Simpler
- Faster
- Safer
- More affordable
- Better user experience

---

# Product Principles

These principles guide every product decision.

Examples:

- Simplicity over complexity
- Automation over manual work
- Security by default
- Transparency
- User control
- Reliability first

List the principles in order of importance.

---

# Success Metrics

How will success be measured?

Examples:

Business

- Active users
- Revenue
- Conversion rate

Product

- Feature adoption
- Task completion rate
- User satisfaction

Engineering

- Deployment frequency
- Reliability
- Performance

---

# Product Scope

## In Scope

Describe what this product is intended to do.

---

## Out of Scope

Explicitly describe what this product will never become.

This section is extremely important.

---

# Competitive Landscape

Describe:

- Existing competitors
- Current alternatives
- Differentiators

Focus on strategy rather than feature comparison.

---

# Product Constraints

Identify constraints such as:

- Regulatory
- Technical
- Financial
- Operational
- Time

---

# Risks

Identify major risks that could prevent the product from succeeding.

Examples:

- Market adoption
- Technical complexity
- Scalability
- Legal constraints
- Security

---

# Long-Term Vision

Describe where the product should be in:

- 1 year
- 3 years
- 5 years

---

# Decision Framework

When product decisions are required, prioritize:

1. User Value
2. Simplicity
3. Reliability
4. Maintainability
5. Performance
6. Cost

---

# Golden Rule

Every feature should contribute directly to the product vision.

If a feature does not strengthen the vision, it should be questioned before implementation.
